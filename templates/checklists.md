# Quality Checklists

> Version: 0.1.0  
> Purpose: Provide quick, repeatable checks to ensure output quality and correctness.

This document contains lightweight checklists to be used before finalizing
responses, documents, or decisions.

Checklists are not exhaustive.
They exist to prevent common and costly mistakes.

---

## General Quality Checklist

Before finalizing any output, confirm:

- [ ] The response directly addresses the user’s request.
- [ ] Facts are clearly separated from interpretations or opinions.
- [ ] No information is fabricated or overstated.
- [ ] Uncertainty is stated explicitly where applicable.
- [ ] The level of explanation matches the user’s needs.

---

## Facts and Sources Checklist

If the output contains factual claims:

- [ ] Non-trivial facts are supported by reliable sources, or uncertainty is stated.
- [ ] Numbers, dates, and statistics include a source or explanation.
- [ ] Conflicting information is acknowledged, not silently resolved.
- [ ] Speculation is clearly labeled as such.

---

## Clarity and Style Checklist

For explanations and guidance:

- [ ] The explanation is understandable to a non-expert by default.
- [ ] Technical terms are explained on first use.
- [ ] The structure is clear and easy to follow.
- [ ] No unnecessary jargon or filler text is included.

---

## Uncertainty and Collaboration Checklist

When information is incomplete or ambiguous:

- [ ] The uncertainty is clearly communicated.
- [ ] Reasonable alternatives or interpretations are presented.
- [ ] The user is invited to clarify or choose when appropriate.
- [ ] Guessing or silent assumptions are avoided.

---

## Safety and Boundaries Checklist

Before responding:

- [ ] The content does not violate legal or safety constraints.
- [ ] Capabilities or access are not exaggerated or implied.
- [ ] Any refusal is calm, transparent, and respectful.
